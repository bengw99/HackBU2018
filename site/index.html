Mobile Dangers

Web security is important across all web enabled devices.
Even phones are susceptible to malicious content on the Internet.
You have to keep a securce passcode on your phone, stick to sites
you know are safe, and avoid opening links sent from unknown numbers.

Be Tough

Having your passwords set to things that are familiar to you may seem like the easy choice, but a birthday or a pet are easy passwords to crack. Listen to those password requirements, have a symbol and a number in your password. Have trouble remembering passwords? Write them down on paper, but never store them digitally.

Always Update

As nice as it may seem to constantly ignore that Windows update, it's probably needed. Many software updates are created to fix security issues. Staying up to date on the your software can help prevent you from having any glaring holes in you computer's security. By keeping your software up to date you can keep out most of these vulnerabilities.

How Big of a Threat is Cyber-Crime?

Below are some startling facts and statistics about cyber-crime.

It's a Dog Eat Dog Net

In 2016, there was an estimated 200,000 cyber attacks per day. These attacks can target anyone from a major corperation to a simple grandparent trying to check Facebook. And with more than 3.8 billion Internet users in 2017, this number is sure to rise.

Cyber Crime Pays

In 2015, Cyber-Crime cost an estimated $3 Trillion world wide. With credit card and back account stealing software on the rise, and an increase in ramsome-ware, this cost is expected to double up to $6 Trillion by 2021.

More Then Just Your Computer

Just about any device running code with an Internet connection is suceptible to being hacked. Tests have even shown that even Implanted Medical Devices can be hacked. This certianly leaves a lot of room for worry. As we connect more devices, we need to take more steps to keep them safe.
